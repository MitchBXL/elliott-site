The distinction

Most AI tells you what to think. Elliott shows you how it got there.

Why it matters

Two kinds of AI reasoning. Only one you can trust.

Discriminative AI

e.g., ChatGPT, most research tools

Pattern-matches across training data to produce an output. Fast. Fluent. Plausible-sounding. But it can't tell you why it reached a conclusion, because it didn't reason to one — it predicted one. The theme appeared. Ask where it came from and you get more words, not more evidence.

Produces outputs without traceable logic
Confident about things it cannot verify
No chain from evidence to conclusion
Hallucination is a feature, not a bug
vs

Causal reasoning

Elliott

Builds a chain from specific evidence to a defensible conclusion. Every observation links to a transcript moment. Every pattern links to the observations behind it. Every interpretation links to the patterns — and the counter-evidence that complicates them. You can interrogate any step. So can your client.

Every claim traces to a specific source
Counter-evidence surfaced, not suppressed
Reasoning is visible and interrogable
Trust is earned by showing its work

How it works

Infrastructure for thinking. Not a shortcut around it.

01

Set up your project

Define your research objectives, your cohorts, and your redaction pattern. Elliott uses this to structure everything that follows. Two days of work doesn't start with a blank page: it starts with a frame.

ObjectivesCohortsRedaction
02

Upload and clean

Drop in your transcripts: VTT, SRT, plain text. Elliott cleans, consolidates fragmented speech, and applies your redaction pattern automatically. Raw transcripts never leave your machine.

Local-firstPrivacy by design
03

Observe and code

Read through your transcripts and mark what you see. Elliott tracks codes across all interviews, suggests consolidation when you use the same concept twice, and keeps observation separate from interpretation.

ObservationField notesCodes
04

Discover patterns — and what breaks them

Elliott surfaces what's consistent across cohorts, what's cohort-specific, and what contradicts your emerging conclusions. It notices when a mindset cuts across demographics. It flags the three participants who said the opposite thing — before a client does.

PatternsCounter-evidenceMindsets
05

Interrogate your own analysis

Ask Elliott to challenge a pattern. Ask why a cohort responded differently. Push back on its observations and it will show you the evidence behind them. Every claim traces back to a specific transcript moment. No orphan insights.
